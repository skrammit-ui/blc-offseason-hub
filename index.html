League Rules
Bush League Chew · Updated Dec 10, 2025
💰 Fees & Teams

League fees are $100 per team per year ($2,000 total). $125 each year goes to Fantrax for premium settings (QA17, MiLB Status, etc). There are 20 total teams.

🏟️ Divisions

Four divisions set each year based on prior-year final seeding (avg seed 10.5 per division). Division names chosen annually by division winners — must be based on an actual MiLB team.

📋 Rosters & Categories

Spots: 22 Active · 8 Reserve · 15 IL · 25 MiLB
Positions: C, 1B, 2B, 3B, SS, MI, CI, OF (×5), UT (×2), P (×9)

📅 Schedule
  • Play every divisional opponent twice (once per half)
  • Play two other divisions — one per half
  • Play the third division once in Week 10 (1v1, 2v2, 3v3…)
🔄 Moves & Trades

Adds: Max 4 per regular season period · Max 2 per playoff period.

Trades: Unlimited. 2/3 majority to overturn. No PTBNLs — all assets exchange at time of trade. Deadline: Aug 10 (end of Week 18). Players retain contract status from draft or FA.

🎯 Draft

Starts March 1st — slow draft format.

  • Rounds 1–15: 4-hour pick clock
  • Rounds 16–30: 2-hour pick clock
  • The round a player is drafted = their Keeper round in future years

Draft order: Consolation teams 9th–20th in order → Championship teams 5–8 in order → Championship teams 1–4 in reverse order.

🆓 Free Agents & Waivers

Free Agents: MLB players added mid-season → Round 15, eligible for 3 additional seasons. Rookie-eligible players → Round 25 (see Prospects).

Waivers: 2-day waiver period. Priority = reverse of original first-round draft order; successful claims move to the bottom. Drafted players cut and re-claimed on waivers retain their round/contract. If they clear waivers and are added as FAs, they get a new Round 15 contract — unless re-added by the same manager who cut them ("Cole Burnes Rule").

⚾ Prospects (MiLB)

25 MiLB roster spots per team. Eligible for players actually in the minors or meeting MLB rookie eligibility (<130 AB and <50 IP). Eligible players show a green M next to their name. Only green-M players may be kept as MiLB players at draft time.

🟣 Rule 5 Draft

Held during the last week of January each year. Each manager protects 35 players regardless of MLB/MiLB status — those players are not eligible for selection. Each manager may select one (1) player from another team. No manager can lose more than two (2) players; once they do, their roster is locked. If the selecting manager decides not to keep the player, they are returned to the original team.

Draft Order: 17th → 18th → 19th → 20th → 16th → 15th → 14th → 13th → 12th → 11th → 10th → 9th → 8th → 7th → 6th → 5th → 4th → 3rd → 2nd → 1st

🔒 Keepers

Each team may keep up to 40 players in the round they were originally drafted (or per Trade/Waiver/FA rules).

  • MLB players: Keepable for max 3 additional seasons after the draft year
  • MiLB players: Keepable while they maintain rookie eligibility; once they lose it, keepable for 4 additional seasons after losing rookie status
  • No rounds 1–5 may be kept — exception: if a manager has multiple 6th-round keepers, they may use a 5th-round slot for one of them; that player retains their 6th-round status
🏆 Playoffs & Payouts

Playoffs begin Week 20, run 5 weeks (final round is 2 weeks). Seeding: division 1st → seeds 1–4 · 2nd → 5–8 · 3rd → 9–12 · 4th → 13–16 · 5th → 17–20.

Week 20 (Wild Card)

  • Division winners get a bye
  • 2nd vs 3rd — Wild Card game
  • 4th vs 5th — Consolation opener

Championship Bracket (Wks 21–24)

  • Wk 21 Divisional: Division winner vs Wild Card winner
  • Wk 22 Championship: Divisional winners (1v4, 2v3)
  • Wks 23–24 World Series: Championship winners play for title

Consolation Bracket (Wks 21–24)

  • Wk 21: 4v5 winner vs Wild Card loser
  • Wk 22: Divisional winners (top vs low, middles vs each other)
  • Wks 23–24 Consolation Final: 9th place + 1st overall pick

Rule 5 Bracket (Wks 21–22)

  • Wk 21: Losers of 4v5 games (top vs low, middles vs each other)
  • Wk 22: Winners play for top Rule 5 Draft selection

Tiebreakers: H2H record → Divisional record → Overall record
